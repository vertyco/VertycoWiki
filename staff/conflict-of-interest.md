# Conflict of Interest Policy

This policy applies to **all staff members equally**, including Admins and the Server Owner. No one is exempt. The integrity of the community depends on every member of Staff being held to the same standard.

A conflict of interest (COI) exists when a staff member's personal connections, relationships, or involvement could compromise, or appear to compromise, their ability to moderate impartially.

> Perception matters. Even if favoritism isn't occurring, the **appearance** of bias is just as damaging to community trust as actual bias. If a significant portion of players believe there is favoritism, recusal is the appropriate response regardless of intent.
{.is-warning}

# When Recusal Is Required

A staff member **must** recuse themselves from moderation actions involving a tribe or player when any of the following apply:

- They are a current or former member of the tribe in question
- They have a close personal relationship with someone in the tribe (close friend, significant other, family member, etc.)
- They are actively involved in a dispute with the tribe or its members
- A significant number of community members have raised concerns about perceived bias toward that tribe or player, even if the bias is not real

> Recusal is not an admission of wrongdoing. It is a tool for protecting both the staff member and the community's trust in fair moderation.
{.is-info}

# What Recusal Means

When recused, a staff member must **not**:

- Issue punishments, warnings, or mod notes involving the tribe or its members
- Handle tickets involving the tribe or its members
- Make or influence decisions about Rshop, VC refunds, or other economy actions for the tribe
- Override another staff member's decision regarding the tribe
- Participate in internal staff discussions about active moderation cases involving the tribe

When a recused staff member receives a report or ticket involving the tribe, they must **immediately** pass it to another uninvolved staff member without weighing in.

# Proactive Disclosure

Staff members should disclose personal connections (tribemates, close friends, family, significant others playing on the server) to the staff team **proactively**, rather than waiting for complaints. This allows the team to route tickets appropriately from the start instead of reacting after drama surfaces.

Disclosures should be posted in the staff channel so the full team is aware.

# Two-Staff Rule for Major Actions

Any significant moderation action involving a tribe connected to **any** staff member requires a second uninvolved staff member to review and approve before it is carried out. This includes but is not limited to:

- Bans or extended timeouts
- Structure wipes or dino removals
- Large VC refunds or economy adjustments

This protects both the staff member and the player from claims of favoritism or retaliation.

# Handling Public Accusations

When players publicly accuse staff of favoritism in chat, do not engage in a debate. Use a standard response along the lines of:

> *"We have a conflict of interest policy. Staff with personal connections to a tribe are recused from handling their cases. If you believe this policy isn't being followed, please open a ticket so we can address your concern properly."*

This acknowledges the concern, points to a concrete policy, and moves the conversation out of public chat where it can spiral.

# Anti-Retaliation

- **Protecting reporters:** Players who raise conflict of interest concerns in good faith through tickets must not face any backlash or negative treatment.
- **Preventing overcorrection:** A recused staff member must not overcompensate by being disproportionately harsh toward the tribe's rivals in an attempt to "prove" neutrality. That is simply bias in the other direction.

# Cool-Down Period

If a staff member leaves a tribe or a personal relationship changes, they should **not** immediately resume moderating cases involving that tribe. A cool-down period of at least **30 days** is recommended before resuming involvement, allowing the community perception to adjust.

# Player Escalation Path

Players should be aware they can raise conflict of interest concerns through the ticket system. If a player believes a staff member has a conflict of interest in their case, they may request a different staff member handle it.

> If you believe a staff member has a conflict of interest in your case, open a ticket and request that a different staff member handle it. These requests are taken seriously and will not result in any negative consequences.
{.is-info}

# Periodic Review

Recusal does not have to be permanent. The admin team should periodically review active recusals (e.g., every few months) to determine whether the underlying concern has been resolved and whether it is appropriate to lift the recusal. Factors to consider:

- Has the personal connection changed?
- Has the community concern subsided?
- Has enough time passed for the perception to reset?

The decision to lift a recusal should be made collectively by the admin team, not unilaterally by the recused staff member.
